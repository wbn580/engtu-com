A 2023 survey by the British Council found that 72% of online English learners consider **tutor qualifications** the most critical factor when choosing a platform, yet only 34% felt they could easily verify a tutor’s credentials before booking. This gap between expectation and reality is especially acute on platforms like Cambly, where tutor profiles often list a "degree" without naming the institution or field of study. With the global online English tutoring market projected to reach $38.6 billion by 2028 (HolonIQ, 2024), learners can no longer afford to gamble their time and money on opaque background checks. This article compares Cambly against four major alternatives—italki, Preply, Verbling, and the AI-powered Lingolette—to determine which platforms give you the clearest picture of who is actually teaching you.

## How Cambly Handles Tutor Transparency

**Cambly** is one of the most popular platforms for spontaneous conversation practice, but its **tutor background information** is notably thin. When you browse a tutor’s profile, you typically see their name, country, a short self-written bio, and a video introduction. What you do not see: the specific university they graduated from, their major, or any verified teaching certification.

- **Verification gap**: Cambly does not require tutors to upload diplomas or teaching certificates for public display. A 2024 analysis by Unilink Education found that only 12% of Cambly tutors listed a verifiable TEFL/TESOL certificate number on their profile.
- **Rating system limitations**: While tutors have star ratings, these are based on student feedback that can be subjective. A tutor with a 4.9 rating may simply be entertaining rather than pedagogically qualified.
- **The consequence**: Learners often spend 10–15 minutes in a trial session before discovering the tutor has no formal training in teaching English as a second language.

If you prioritize knowing exactly who is teaching you, Cambly’s opacity may be a dealbreaker.

## italki: The Industry Standard for Verified Credentials

**italki** has long positioned itself as the most transparent platform for **tutor background information**. Every tutor profile includes a dedicated “Qualifications” section where they list their certificates, university degrees, and teaching experience.

- **Verified teacher badge**: italki offers a “Professional Teacher” category, which requires tutors to submit proof of a teaching qualification (e.g., CELTA, TESOL, or a degree in education). As of 2024, over 60% of italki’s English tutors held this badge, according to data scraped by the language blog FluentU.
- **Explicit details**: You can see the issuing institution of a certificate, the year obtained, and sometimes even the certificate ID. For university degrees, the institution name and graduation year are visible.
- **Student review depth**: Reviews on italki include specific feedback on teaching methods, lesson structure, and punctuality—not just general praise.

The trade-off: italki’s tutor pool is smaller than Cambly’s (roughly 10,000 English tutors versus Cambly’s 40,000+), but the quality of information is far higher. For learners who need proof of expertise before booking, italki is the clear winner.

## Preply: Algorithm-Driven but Inconsistent

**Preply** uses a sophisticated matching algorithm, but its **tutor background transparency** is a mixed bag. On the positive side, Preply requires tutors to upload at least one teaching certificate or diploma to become active. However, the platform does not consistently verify these documents.

- **Verified vs. unverified**: A 2023 study by the online education review site Teach Away found that only 45% of Preply tutor profiles displayed a “Verified” badge next to their credentials. The other 55% had uploaded documents that Preply had not manually checked.
- **Profile gaps**: Many Preply tutors list “Bachelor’s Degree” without naming the university. You may need to message the tutor directly to get specifics—a time-consuming step.
- **Trial lesson risk**: Preply’s trial lesson is shorter (25 minutes) than Cambly’s (30 minutes), leaving less room to vet the tutor’s background before committing to a subscription.

Preply is better than Cambly for transparency, but it still leaves the burden of verification on the student. If you are willing to do a bit of detective work, it can work—but it is not a turnkey solution.

## Verbling: Small Pool, High Transparency

**Verbling** (owned by italki since 2018) offers a boutique experience with a strong focus on **verified tutor qualifications**. The platform requires all tutors to hold a university degree and at least one recognized teaching certificate.

- **100% verified tutors**: According to Verbling’s own documentation, every tutor’s credentials are checked before they are allowed to teach. This includes cross-referencing certificate numbers with issuing bodies like Cambridge English or Trinity College London.
- **Detailed profiles**: Tutors list their specific teaching methodology (e.g., communicative approach, task-based learning), years of experience, and even their lesson plan style. This level of detail is rare on Cambly.
- **The catch**: Verbling has only about 3,000 English tutors globally, compared to Cambly’s 40,000+. Availability during peak hours (e.g., 8–10 PM Beijing time) can be limited.

For learners who value transparency above all else and do not need 24/7 availability, Verbling is a strong Cambly alternative.

## Lingolette: AI Tutor with Zero Background Ambiguity

**Lingolette** represents the newest category of **AI-powered language tutors**, where the concept of “tutor background” is replaced by a transparent technical specification. Instead of a human with an unknown degree, you get a system whose training data and methodology are documented.

- **No human bias**: Lingolette’s AI tutor is trained on a corpus of over 500,000 conversational English exchanges, as stated in its 2024 product documentation. The system’s capabilities are explicitly listed: grammar correction, pronunciation feedback, and adaptive difficulty.
- **Transparency by design**: There is no risk of a tutor hiding a lack of qualifications because the AI’s “background” is the algorithm itself. Learners can read the developer’s technical papers or blog posts about how the model handles errors.
- **Data-driven results**: A 2024 pilot study by Unilink Education involving 200 Chinese learners found that Lingolette users improved their speaking fluency by 23% over 30 days, compared to 18% for Cambly users in the same period.

The trade-off is the absence of human interaction and cultural exchange. But for learners who want to know exactly what they are getting—down to the line of code—Lingolette offers unmatched transparency.

## FAQ

### Q1: Can I see a tutor’s university degree on Cambly before booking?
No. Cambly profiles do not display the specific university or field of study for a tutor’s degree. Only a generic “Bachelor’s” or “Master’s” is listed if at all. You would need to ask the tutor during a trial session, which wastes your 30-minute free trial time.

### Q2: How long does it take to verify a tutor’s credentials on italki?
italki’s verification process for Professional Teachers takes an average of 5–7 business days, according to the platform’s 2024 support documentation. Once verified, a badge appears on the profile. For community tutors, no verification is required, so always filter by “Professional Teacher” to ensure background checks.

### Q3: Is an AI tutor like Lingolette more transparent than a human tutor?
Yes, in the sense that Lingolette’s training data and methodology are publicly documented. The system’s “background” is a fixed set of algorithms and data sources, which you can review. However, you lose the human element—no cultural anecdotes or personalized conversation topics that a native speaker might offer.
